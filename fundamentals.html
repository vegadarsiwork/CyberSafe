Cybersecurity Fundamentals

Learn the basics of cybersecurity and stay protected online.

Common Cybersecurity Threats

Understanding these common cybersecurity threats is crucial to maintaining your digital safety:

  • Phishing: Scams where attackers pose as trusted entities (like banks or online services) to steal personal information. Be cautious of unsolicited emails or messages asking for sensitive details.
  • Malware: Malicious software that can infect your devices. It includes viruses, spyware, and trojans. Avoid downloading files or clicking links from unknown sources.
  • Ransomware: A type of malware that locks your files, demanding payment for access. Regularly back up your data to reduce the impact of potential ransomware attacks.

Creating Strong Passwords and Securing Accounts

Protecting your accounts is a fundamental step toward cybersecurity:

  • Strong Passwords: Use passwords that are long and complex, containing a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Avoid common phrases or predictable patterns.
  • Recognizing Scams: Be wary of any unexpected requests for personal information. Scams often impersonate trusted sources and use urgency to prompt a quick response.
  • Securing Accounts: Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible. This adds an extra layer of protection, requiring a second form of verification to access your account.

Types of Cybersecurity Attacks

There are various types of cybersecurity attacks that beginners should be aware of:

  • Brute Force Attacks: Attackers attempt to guess your password by trying various combinations. Strong, unique passwords can make this much more difficult.
  • Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks: Attackers flood a network or service with excessive requests, causing it to crash. This is often used to disrupt services temporarily.
  • Social Engineering: Tactics used to manipulate individuals into giving away confidential information, often by creating a false sense of trust.
