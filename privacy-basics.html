Privacy Basics

Your one-stop shop for tips protecting your personal privacy online.

Privacy on Social Media

Understand the importance of managing privacy on social platforms like Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, and LinkedIn. Here are some key steps to reduce data exposure:

  • Privacy Settings: Each platform has its own privacy settings. Review and adjust these to control who can see your posts, photos, and personal details.
  • Location Sharing: Disable location sharing in apps to prevent platforms from tagging your posts with your real-time location, which can compromise your privacy.
  • Online Status: Many platforms allow you to manage your online visibility. Adjust these settings to limit who can see when you're online or your last active status.

Using VPNs

Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) help protect your browsing data and enhance online privacy. Here’s how you can get started with VPNs:

  • Setting Up a VPN: Choose a VPN provider and install their software on your devices. Follow the setup instructions to start using a secure, encrypted connection.
  • Choosing Reliable VPN Providers: Look for reputable VPN providers with no-logs policies, strong encryption, and fast servers. Popular options include NordVPN, ExpressVPN, and CyberGhost.
  • Using VPNs on Public Wi-Fi: Public Wi-Fi networks are vulnerable to cyber threats. VPNs secure your internet connection, making it safer to use public Wi-Fi at places like cafes or airports.

Securing Personal Data

Protecting sensitive information stored on your devices is critical to privacy. Use these methods to secure your personal data:

  • Strong Passwords: Use complex passwords, combining uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Avoid using the same password across multiple sites.
  • Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Enable 2FA for an added layer of security, requiring a code sent to your phone or email in addition to your password.
  • Data Encryption: Encrypt sensitive files or use apps with built-in encryption. This makes your files unreadable to unauthorized users even if they gain access.

Quick Tips

Here are a few easy steps to reduce data collection and improve online privacy:

  • Disable Ad Tracking: Go to your phone settings and disable ad tracking to limit advertisers’ access to your browsing data.
  • Clear Browser Cookies: Regularly delete cookies and clear your browser history to remove stored data that can be used to track you.
  • Limit Location Data Sharing: Only allow apps access to your location when absolutely necessary, and turn off location services when not in use.
