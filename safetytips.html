Safety Tips

Practical steps to help you stay safe online.

Creating Strong Passwords

Secure your accounts by creating complex, unique passwords for each service. Here are some tips:

  • Length: Aim for at least 12 characters, mixing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
  • Unique Passwords: Avoid using the same password for multiple accounts.
  • Password Managers: Use a password manager to keep track of passwords securely.

Checklist:

  • ✅ My passwords are at least 12 characters long.
  • ✅ I use unique passwords for each account.
  • ✅ I store passwords in a secure password manager.

Recognizing Phishing Attempts

Phishing scams are deceptive emails or messages that try to steal your personal information. Protect yourself by:

  • Checking the Sender: Verify the sender’s email address carefully.
  • Avoiding Suspicious Links: Hover over links to check where they lead before clicking.
  • Looking for Red Flags: Be cautious of emails that request urgent action or contain poor grammar.

Checklist:

  • ✅ I double-check sender addresses in emails.
  • ✅ I avoid clicking on unverified links.
  • ✅ I am cautious of urgent or suspicious messages.

Using Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Add an extra layer of security to your accounts by enabling two-factor authentication. Here’s how it helps:

  • Added Security: 2FA requires a second form of verification, such as a code sent to your phone.
  • Easy Setup: Most platforms offer 2FA in their account settings.
  • Different Types: Use app-based 2FA for better security than SMS-based methods.

Checklist:

  • ✅ I have enabled 2FA on all critical accounts.
  • ✅ I use app-based 2FA where possible.
  • ✅ I regularly update my 2FA methods.

  • Password Security: Remember to update passwords periodically and avoid reuse.
